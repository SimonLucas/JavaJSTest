Hello World Evolved

A simple interactive introduction to evolutionary algorithms

Evolutionary algorithms (EAs) provide a simple yet powerful and general approach to many problems in AI. While EAs have been the subject of decades of fruitful research, it is also important to understand that great results can often be achieved with very simple EAs. In this article we'll study the simplest possible EA, and show how it can be applied to a design problem that most other AI approaches would I think struggle to solve in so simple a manner.

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]

In any EA we need to define the following:

  • A way to represent solutions. For example, a vector of numbers.
  • A way to copy and vary a solution. For example, copy a vector numbers, but randomly change some in the copying process.
  • A way to evaluate the value (fitness) of a solution. This can be defined in absolute terms (e.g. how fast can it move?) or in relative terms (can solution A beat solution B in football?).
